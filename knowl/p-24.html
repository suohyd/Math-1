If we put
\begin{equation*} A=\begin{pmatrix}a_{11}\amp a_{12}\amp\cdots\amp a_{1n}\\a_{21}\amp a_{22}\amp\cdots\amp a_{2n}\\\vdots\amp\vdots\amp\ddots\amp\vdots\\a_{m1}\amp a_{m2}\amp\cdots\amp a_{mn}\end{pmatrix} \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} X=\begin{pmatrix}x_1\\x_2\\\vdots\\x_n\end{pmatrix}\text{,} \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} B=\begin{pmatrix}b_1\\b_2\\\vdots\\b_m\end{pmatrix} \end{equation*}
then, the above system of linear equations can be written as
\begin{equation*} AX=B. \end{equation*}
The matrix \(A\) is called the coefficient matrix of the above system.
