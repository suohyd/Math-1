Convention 1.1.3.

We denote the set of all \(n\times n\) matrices over \(\mathbb{R}\) by \(M_n(\mathbb{R})\text{.}\) So \(M_{n\times n}(\mathbb{R})=M_n(\mathbb{R})\text{.}\) For any matrix \(A\in M_{m\times n}(\mathbb{R})\) and \(\alpha\in\mathbb{R}\) we denote by \(\alpha A\in M_{m\times n}(\mathbb{R})\) the matrix obtained by multiplying every entry of the matrix \(A\) by \(\alpha\text{.}\)
